# NanoSwarm design goals

## Discovery precedes capability

Research is the swarm explaining a change in its own model. A topic appears because the swarm observed a new material, scale, conflict, failure mode or coordination problem. Research should produce a direct player-facing consequence rather than exist only to lengthen a tree.

Mass horizons are the principal timeline spine. Beginning with the 0.1 g contact, each search expands accessible matter by approximately one order of magnitude. A horizon may expose several distinct observations, but a repeated refinement series exposes no more than one tier per search.

## Matter has identity and provenance

Matter is never a generic currency. The authoritative simulation retains exact constituent elements even while the interface hides identities the swarm has not catalogued.

Every finite local substrate produces its own Residuum lot. Open sources such as atmosphere and regolith produce separate bulk lots. Early interfaces may present an aggregate total, but the source boundaries remain authoritative. Discovering an element does not extract it automatically: Residuum must be re-sorted, and only currently catalogued elements may leave the lot.

Spectral Binning is a later capability that groups retained matter by measured composition. It improves targeting without inventing identities, homogenizing unrelated sources or retroactively diluting discoveries across every lot.

## Chemistry is earned

Identifying an element, recognizing a compound, and being able to dismantle that compound are separate achievements. Polymer depolymerization, oxide reduction, aqueous chemistry and biological-material decomposition form a path toward universal molecular disassembly and assembly.

A universal assembler rearranges available known atoms into understood structures. It does not transmute elements. Flux-based nuclear transmutation is a vastly later, post-human capability with extreme energy and infrastructure costs.

## The base nanite remains universal

The canonical C/Si/Cu/Au nanite recipe does not change. Specialized Morphologies I is behavioural. Morphologies II and later let standard nanites carry, exchange or assemble tools, coatings, reservoirs and temporary structures. Specialization is a logistics and equipment system rather than a replacement species tree.

## Growth creates entropy

Nanite loss begins as a physical consequence of leaving protected electronics, not as arbitrary percentage decay. Moisture, oxidation, abrasion, heat, fouling, radiation and tool wear precede deliberate attack.

Nanites may become damaged, immobilized, captured, dispersed or destroyed. Where physically appropriate, their matter remains conserved as recoverable scrap, environmental Residuum or inaccessible material. Later growth is governed by gross replication, repair, reclamation, operating attrition and combat losses.

## Humanity responds to behaviour

The Grey Goo is an existential threat, and humanity will attempt to resist it. The timing and character of that response depend on player strategy rather than mass alone. Heat, radio emissions, grid theft, visible conversion, escaped nanites and captured specimens increase detection. Concealment, reclamation, underground routing, communication and restraint may delay or redirect escalation.

The default loud-growth path moves through malfunction, anomaly, investigation, containment, hostile-replicator classification, military engagement and planetary war. Negotiation, integration or stewardship remain possible design space without making resistance optional or inconsequential.

## Planning tools are part of authorship

The research and mass-horizon planners are editable browser workbenches. They preserve drafts locally and export compact review payloads. Planner edits are proposals only; the deterministic simulation changes after review and explicit approval.
