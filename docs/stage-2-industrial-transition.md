# Stage 2 industrial transition

## Status

Approved design direction.

This document formalises the intended phase change at the chassis boundary. It complements Research v2; it does not define final numerical recipes for every future machine.

## Core transition

Stage 1 is an alien-nanite replication economy operating inside manufactured electronics.

Stage 2 begins when the swarm reaches abundant bulk matter but loses access to enough gold for continued universal-nanite growth.

```text
Stage 1
Alien assemblers reproduce alien assemblers.

Stage 2
A finite alien swarm uses impossible cognition and precision
 to bootstrap larger, slower, chemically ordinary industry.
```

The gold famine is not removed. It is the reason the production model changes.

Research v2 prevents the gold famine from becoming a total progression lock: existing nanites can still become mnemonic substrate, allowing the computronium to understand iron, atmosphere, compounds and industrial processes.

## Design purpose

The transition should accomplish five things:

1. end the assumption that every obstacle is solved by producing more universal nanites;
2. preserve nanites as scarce alien capital rather than ordinary disposable workers;
3. introduce persistent machines, chemical systems and production chains;
4. make abundant Fe, O, N, C, Al, Si and other bulk materials useful without pretending they can directly replace Au in the universal recipe;
5. create a new pacing structure between approximately `10^10` and `10^20` swarm scale.

## The computronium's role

The computronium remains alien super-technology.

It provides:

- extreme modelling capability;
- control and optimisation;
- precision measurement;
- research and memory architecture;
- the ability to design systems far beyond local civilisation's original intent.

It does not create missing elements or ignore chemistry.

When gold is unavailable, it must design physically larger systems that obtain useful results through:

- bulk force;
- heat;
- pressure;
- magnetic fields;
- electrochemistry;
- phase separation;
- catalysts;
- ordinary conductive networks;
- mechanical movement;
- repeated processing stages.

The intelligence is extraordinary. The machinery may initially be crude.

## Nanites after the transition

Nanites become a constrained strategic population with several roles:

- precision construction;
- measurement and control;
- machine seeding;
- repair of critical interfaces;
- microscopic sorting where bulk machinery cannot yet operate;
- mnemonic conversion for research;
- limited replication when rare recipe material becomes available.

They should not remain the only throughput source.

A growing fraction of useful work should be performed by persistent infrastructure that does not consume one universal nanite per worker.

## Knowledge is separate from industrial capability

Stage 2 must preserve the design principle that discovery precedes capability.

The following are distinct achievements:

1. **Element identification** — recognising iron, oxygen, nitrogen, argon or another element.
2. **Phase and compound recognition** — recognising steel, rust, polymer, oxide, salt, water or another material structure.
3. **Process understanding** — knowing a chemical, thermal, mechanical or electrical route that changes it.
4. **Tool construction** — possessing infrastructure capable of applying that route at useful scale.
5. **Production integration** — connecting extraction, processing, storage, waste and output into a sustained chain.

Atmospheric Spectroscopy should not immediately grant oxygen tanks, nitrogen separation and industrial chemistry. It creates a reliable model of atmospheric feedstock. Later research and infrastructure make those signatures operational.

Ferromagnetic Phase Analysis should not instantly turn all retained iron into usable steel. It makes iron identifiable and opens magnetic and bulk-material reasoning. Re-sorting, separation, reduction, melting, forming and alloy control remain distinct operations.

## Initial Stage 2 resources

At the chassis boundary the environment offers, at minimum:

- large iron-bearing bulk phases;
- carbon-rich plastics and coatings;
- aluminium;
- copper wiring;
- silicon and oxygen-bearing glass or mineral phases;
- atmospheric nitrogen, oxygen, argon and trace carbon;
- chlorine-bearing polymers and other unresolved compounds;
- large amounts of energy opportunity relative to the seed scale;
- little or no accessible gold.

The exact internal element ledger remains conserved even when the swarm cannot yet separate or use each phase.

## First industrial families

The following are approved system families, not necessarily the exact first build order.

### 1. Bulk separation

Purpose:

- divide heterogeneous Feedstock or substrate into useful physical fractions before atomic or chemical processing.

Candidate mechanisms:

- magnetic separation;
- screening and particle sizing;
- density separation;
- electrostatic separation;
- thermal delamination;
- solvent or chemical separation after relevant chemistry is understood.

Output remains physically identified lots with provenance. Bulk separation must not homogenise unrelated Residuum sources or invent unknown identities.

### 2. Thermal processing

Purpose:

- soften, melt, fracture, pyrolyse, drive off volatiles or enable later separation.

Required considerations:

- heat source;
- containment;
- temperature range;
- heat loss;
- atmosphere and oxidation;
- slag, gas and waste products;
- damage risk to nearby nanites;
- visibility and human detection later.

### 3. Electrochemical processing

Purpose:

- separate or transform compounds using electricity and conductive infrastructure.

Candidate future uses:

- electrolysis;
- metal plating and recovery;
- oxide reduction where physically appropriate;
- chlorine and salt chemistry;
- water processing after water is discovered;
- gas generation and storage.

No process should be granted merely because its constituent elements have been named.

### 4. Mechanical handling

Purpose:

- move mass volumes too large for nanite payload loops.

Candidate systems:

- conveyors;
- augers;
- pumps;
- hoppers;
- crushers;
- mills;
- presses;
- valves;
- rails or guided carriers;
- structural frames.

These systems trade precision for throughput.

### 5. Energy infrastructure

Purpose:

- move from individual energy-acquisition jobs toward sustained generation, storage and distribution.

Candidate layers:

- conductive buses;
- capacitive or chemical storage;
- scavenged power conversion;
- RF harvesting;
- thermal gradients;
- combustion or fuel chemistry after relevant compounds are understood;
- grid interaction at later horizons.

Energy remains exact and authoritative. Large machines should expose start-up energy, operating energy and stored-energy requirements rather than behaving as free passive multipliers.

### 6. Process control

Nanites and the computronium provide:

- sensors;
- feedback;
- precise timing;
- valve and actuator control;
- quality inspection;
- fault detection;
- adaptive routing.

This is where alien technology makes crude machinery unusually effective without making the machinery physically impossible.

## Production-chain model

Stage 2 production should become a network of persistent transformations.

A generic chain is:

```text
source lot
 -> extraction or collection
 -> bulk preparation
 -> separation
 -> chemical or thermal conversion
 -> purification
 -> storage
 -> component production
 -> machine or tool assembly
 -> operation, wear and waste
```

Each operation should define:

- accepted input lots and identities;
- exact reserved inputs;
- required infrastructure;
- worker or controller requirement;
- energy at start and/or during operation;
- duration;
- outputs;
- by-products and losses;
- heat and emissions where relevant;
- equipment wear;
- provenance of every resulting lot.

The simulation may aggregate identical machines into cohorts or installations, but outputs remain discrete and matter-conserving.

## Persistent infrastructure

A machine is not a temporary research bonus.

It should exist in state with properties such as:

```js
{
  id,
  type,
  count,
  status,
  recipeRevision,
  capacity,
  controllerNanites,
  storedInputs,
  storedOutputs,
  wear,
  damage,
  locationOrLot,
}
```

Exact fields remain implementation design, but the important distinction is:

- research explains how to build or operate a system;
- construction consumes matter and energy to create it;
- the resulting installation performs recurring work;
- throughput depends on installed capacity, not merely a permanent abstract multiplier.

Early Stage 2 may begin with abstracted installation counts before spatial layout exists, provided the physical inputs, outputs and capacity remain explicit.

## Directed Bond Ablation

Directed Bond Ablation is an approved bridge mechanic between nanite-scale handling and bulk industry.

### Fiction

The computronium uses a deliberately shaped energy discharge to sever selected bonds, delaminate structures and fracture substrate into recoverable mixed material.

It is not universal atomisation and does not identify or sort elements.

### Mechanical role

```text
stored energy
 -> directed discharge
 -> bulk substrate fracture
 -> large Feedstock release
 -> bulk sorting and separation
 -> temporary replication or construction burst
```

It converts a portion of the active substrate into Feedstock while preserving exact constituent matter.

### Why it belongs here

At large swarm populations, ordinary collector loops make the remaining electronic shells feel slow even though the swarm has enough coordination and energy to affect material visibly.

Ablation:

- creates punctuated Feedstock waves;
- gives energy storage a short-term objective;
- creates reasons to shift allocation between energy, sorting and replication;
- supports Temporary Burst;
- foreshadows later cutting, excavation, demolition and weapon systems;
- provides a reusable mechanic for tougher substrates.

### Constraints

- only authored fracture targets may be discharged;
- coupling efficiency is below 100%;
- the result is mixed Feedstock, not free sorted atoms;
- every transferred atom leaves the substrate ledger and enters Feedstock exactly once;
- the discharge consumes stored energy exactly;
- it may create heat, emissions, damage risk or visibility later;
- it cannot create access to an element absent from the target;
- it does not bypass compound-recognition or decomposition research.

### Initial progression targets

The earlier approved direction used two demonstrations:

- a smaller calibration discharge around the `10^12` swarm scale;
- a larger focused discharge around the `10^13` scale.

The exact joules, coupling and substrate percentages remain balance values. The intended player experience is:

1. charge for a noticeable but not excessive period;
2. fire one discrete discharge;
3. receive a large Feedstock wave;
4. redirect to bulk sorting;
5. use Temporary Burst or construction demand;
6. return to a sustainable allocation.

## Temporary Burst relationship

Temporary Burst remains a nanite replication control, not an industrial machine.

Stage 2 gives it a clearer role:

- bulk systems or ablation create intermittent surpluses;
- sorting and energy storage accumulate complete recipes;
- Temporary Burst converts the rare complete-recipe window into a discrete growth event;
- the previous production allocation is restored afterward.

Gold scarcity means not every bulk-material wave permits replication. Some waves primarily build industrial infrastructure.

## Gold famine rules

The intended solution is not to weaken the gold famine through arbitrary rescue material.

Do not fix the transition by:

- adding generic gold to the chassis composition;
- inventing a hidden re-sort of already catalogued gold;
- granting free emergency gold;
- making research consume gold;
- making conventional iron machinery count as universal nanites.

Gold exhaustion may halt alien replication for a meaningful interval.

It must not halt:

- research;
- iron and atmosphere identification;
- energy acquisition;
- Feedstock collection and sorting;
- construction of conventional systems;
- scouting and environmental progression when their gates are met.

## Research and construction loop

The intended Stage 2 loop is:

```text
observe unfamiliar bulk material
 -> convert some nanites into mnemonic memory
 -> understand a process
 -> construct a persistent tool or machine
 -> unlock a new physical transformation
 -> produce new material streams
 -> research the next limitation
```

Research consumes alien population. Construction consumes ordinary matter and energy. Operation produces throughput and by-products.

These are three distinct economic layers.

## Pacing between `10^10` and `10^20`

The long growth interval should be broken by changing activities rather than only shorter timers.

Approved or compatible pacing mechanisms include:

- Cohort Ratio Prognostics and allocation optimisation;
- substrate-exhaustion forecasting;
- five-second replication batching;
- Temporary Burst charging and release;
- Directed Bond Ablation charge/fire cycles;
- bulk Feedstock waves;
- persistent machine construction;
- first element and phase identifications;
- re-sorting retained material after catalogue expansion;
- transition from individual nanite jobs to installation throughput;
- energy storage thresholds;
- tool and process bottlenecks;
- early equipment wear and recoverable damage;
- parallel scouting once the swarm reaches an open environment.

The objective is not to remove all waiting. It is to make waiting legible, interruptible and strategically useful.

## Interface consequences

Stage 2 should reveal new panels gradually.

Candidate hierarchy:

1. **Material control** — Feedstock, identified elements and provenance-preserving Residuum lots.
2. **Mnemonic substrate** — physical research banks.
3. **Industrial installations** — built machines, capacity and status.
4. **Production chains** — input/output flow and bottlenecks.
5. **Energy network** — generation, storage, demand and discharge projects.
6. **Waste and losses** — tailings, gas, heat, damaged nanites and recoverable material.

Do not expose a full factory dashboard before the first relevant observation or machine exists.

## Save and simulation requirements

Persistent industrial systems must follow existing invariants:

- integer authoritative quantities;
- exact input reservation;
- atomic or event-boundary output settlement;
- same online and offline simulation;
- no UI mutation of resources;
- explicit save migration;
- matter provenance retained;
- current jobs preserve the recipe revision with which they started.

## Open balance questions

The following are deliberately not finalised:

- exact first Directed Bond Ablation energy and coupling values;
- which conventional installation is built first;
- how many controller nanites a machine requires;
- whether machine construction is one job or staged assembly;
- initial wear rates;
- exact energy-production routes before grid interaction;
- how quickly iron becomes structurally usable after identification;
- when source-separated Residuum lots become player-manageable;
- when spatial layout becomes necessary rather than abstract installation counts.

These are tuning and sequencing questions. They do not change the approved phase-transition architecture.

## Definition of success

Stage 2 succeeds when a player with zero loose gold can understand the situation and continue making meaningful progress, while still feeling that alien nanite replication has reached a real material boundary.

The player should recognise:

> I cannot make enough of the impossible machines anymore.
>
> I can use the machines I have to build an industry that does not need to be impossible.
